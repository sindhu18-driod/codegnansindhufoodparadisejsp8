Actually the law provides a variety of avenues to you in most countries. There are several charges which can be brought against the accused party if non vegetarian articles are sold under the guide of vegetarian food. These are covered as being under the Consumer Protection Acts in that you get what you are promised as part of the service; and also under misrepresentation as an item not being what it is described to be. Also, in some countries, it has religious connotations as hurting the religious sentiments of a party etc.But you would also need to look for indications or terms and conditions to ensure that the item being sold to you is indeed being claimed to be vegetarian to have a legal argument in your favor. Some items which seem to comprise of actively vegetarian ingredients may have additives which contain non vegetarian ingredients like say fish oil, mayo made from eggs, animal fat etc, prawn paste. and these might have been indicated in the fine print.
